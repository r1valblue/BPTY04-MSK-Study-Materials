ARTICULATION/JOINT
• CONNECTION BETWEEN TWO BONES IN THE SKELETON. THE JOINT
IS THE CENTER OF MOTION OF A BONE IN RELATION TO ANOTHER.
JOINT CAN BE CLASSIFIED ACCORDING TO:
1. STRUCTURE
2. MOBILITY
3. RANGE OF MOTION

1. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: STRUCTURE
C. SYNOVIAL JOINT
Most common joint in the human body
CHARACTERISTIC FEATURES:
- Articular capsule
a. Outer fibrous layer >> to stabilize
b. Inner synovial layer (in orange) >> absorbs and secretes
synovial fluid in joint cavity to lubricate the joint
- Articular Surface
a. Covered in hyaline (articular) cartilage (in light blue) >> to
reduce friction and shock absorption
=== PAGE 14 ===
1. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: STRUCTURE
C. SYNOVIAL JOINT
Most common joint in the human body
ADDITIONAL FEATURES:
- Articular discs (e.g., medial and lateral menisci in the knee)
- Bursae
a. Small sacs lined by the synovial membrane and filled with synovial fluid >> to reduce
friction caused by muscles and tendons which are located over bony joint

2. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: MOBILITY
A. DIARTHROSES >> FREELY MOBILE JOINT >> EVERY SYNOVIAL JOINT
B. AMPHIARTHROSES >> SLIGHTLY MOBILE JOINT >> e.g., PUBIC SYMPHISIS AND SYNDESMOSIS
C. SYNARTHROSES >> IMMOBILE JOINT >> e.g., SUTURE OF THE SKULL, GOMPOSHIS, AND
SYNCHONDROSIS
NOTE: ALTHOUGH A JOINT MAY SLIGHTLY MOVE, IT DOES NOT PERFORM A FUNCTIONAL MOVEMENT

=== PAGE 18 ===
3. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: RANGE OF MOTION
A. MULTIAXIAL >> BALL AND SOCKET JOINT
One ball shaped and one socket-like articular surface
e.g., ACETABULOFEMORAL AND GLENOHUMERAL JOINTS
MOVEMENTS:
- FLEXION AND EXTESION (FRONTAL AXIS)
- ABDUCTION AND ADDUCTION (SAGITTAL AXIS)
- INTERNAL AND EXTERNAL ROTATION (VERTICAL AXIS)

=== PAGE 20 ===
3. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: RANGE OF MOTION

=== PAGE 21 ===
3. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: RANGE OF MOTION
C. BIAXIAL >> CONDYLAR (ALSO, ELLIPSOID) JOINT
e.g., RADIOCARPAL JOINT
MOVEMENTS:
- RADIAL AND ULNAR DEVIATION (SAGITTAL AXIS)
- FLEXION AND EXTESION (FRONTAL AXIS)
=== PAGE 22 ===
3. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: RANGE OF MOTION
C. BIAXIAL >> SADDLE JOINT
The bones meet at the concave articular surface of one bone and the
convex articular surface of the other that form the saddle shape
MOVEMENTS:
- ABDUCTION AND ADDUCTION (SAGITTAL AXIS)
- FLEXION AND EXTESION (FRONTAL AXIS)
- CIRCUMDUCTION
=== PAGE 23 ===
3. JOINT CLASSIFICATION: RANGE OF MOTION
D. PLANE JOINT
Do not move around an axis and permit movement along the plane of the
articular surface. Therefore, cannot be classified.
e.g., Acromioclavicular joint, spinal zygapophyseal joint
MOVEMENTS:
- GLIDING OR SLIDING (when one bone moves across the surface of
another)
