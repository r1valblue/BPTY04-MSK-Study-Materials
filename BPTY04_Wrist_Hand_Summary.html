🔗 Radiocarpal Joint
Type: Ellipsoid joint · Biplanar · Radius articulates with proximal carpal bones (excluding pisiform) · Has articular disc

Ligaments

LigamentOriginInsertionFunction
Palmar radiocarpal lig.Anterior radiusScaphoid, lunate, triquetrumSagittal plane stability
Dorsal radiocarpal lig.Posterior radiusScaphoid, lunate, triquetrumSagittal plane stability
Radial collateral lig.––Limits wrist abduction (radial deviation)
Ulnar collateral lig.––Limits wrist adduction (ulnar deviation)

Carpal Tunnel

Flexor retinaculum creates the carpal tunnel. Contains: median nerve + flexor tendons.
⚠️ Carpal Tunnel Syndrome: compression of the median nerve causing paraesthesia in lateral 3½ digits.

🔗 Carpometacarpal (CMC) Joints

1st CMC (Thumb)

Type: Saddle joint · 2 planes of motion + rotation → enables opposition
Ligament groupFunction
Lateral ligamentsSide-to-side stability; limit abduction and adduction; stabilize during pinch
Palmar (volar) ligamentsLimit extension; guide flexion and rotation during opposition
Dorsal ligamentsPrimary stabilizers; prevent dorsal displacement during pinch, grip, opposition

2nd–5th CMC (Fingers)

Type: Plane joints · Very little movement
