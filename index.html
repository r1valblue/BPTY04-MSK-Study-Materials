How to use these resources

Summary Concise, image-embedded overview of each topic. Use this for a quick review before a lecture, tutorial, or to refresh material you already know.
Flashcards Anki deck (.apkg) with Q&A, cloze, and image identification cards. Use this for active recall practice — the best method for long-term retention of muscle attachments, joint types, and nerve supply.
In-Depth Scholarly explanatory guide going beyond the slides — anatomical reasoning, clinical applications, mnemonics, and the "why" behind the facts. Use this when you want to understand material, not just memorise it, or to prepare for case-based assessments.
Practice Q&A Self-testing question set with hidden reveal answers — Recall, Application, and Clinical categories. Use this to consolidate knowledge after a lecture or summary review, check gaps before an exam, or simulate case-based reasoning without the passive reading of flashcards.

How to import flashcard decks into Anki

  1. Download the free Anki app on your computer, phone, or tablet.
  2. Click Flashcards on any topic to download the .apkg file.
  3. Open Anki → File → Import → select the downloaded file. All images are bundled inside — no extra setup needed.
  4. To sync across devices, create a free account at AnkiWeb and enable sync.
