🔗 Arthrology – Sternoclavicular (SC) Joint
Type: Saddle joint · Biplanar + rotation movements · Has an articular disk

Ligaments

  • Interclavicular ligament – connects both clavicles
  • Sternoclavicular ligament – reinforces the capsule
  • Costoclavicular ligament – limits elevation

🔗 Arthrology – Scapulothoracic (ST) Joint
Not a true synovial joint. Functional articulation between the anterior scapula and superolateral posterior thoracic wall.

Slide 9: Scapulothoracic joint

🔗 Arthrology – Acromioclavicular (AC) Joint
Type: Plane joint · Multiaxial – 3 degrees of freedom

Movements

Protraction/Retraction · Elevation/Depression (15° total) · Lateral/Medial rotation (30° total)

Ligaments

LigamentFunction
Acromioclavicular lig.Reinforces articular capsule; stabilizes in all directions
Coracoacromial lig.Prevents upward dislocation of GH joint
Coracoclavicular lig. (Trapezoid + Conoid)Prevents dislocation of AC joint

🔗 Arthrology – Glenohumeral (GH) Joint
Type: Spheroidal (ball and socket) · Shallow socket → highly mobile but unstable

Glenohumeral Ligaments

LigamentOriginInsertion
Superior GHLSuperior glenoid cavityLesser tubercle of humerus
Middle GHLAnterior glenoid cavityAnatomical neck of humerus
Inferior GHLInferior glenoid cavitySurgical neck of humerus

Stabilising Structures

  • Coracohumeral ligament
  • Glenoid labrum – fibrocartilaginous ring that deepens the socket
  • Articular capsule
  • Multiple bursae + Transverse humeral ligament
